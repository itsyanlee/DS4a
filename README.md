# The Academic Impact of COVID-19
### An Empirical Analysis of NYC's Five Largest School Districts

A collaborative data science research project completed through the **Correlation One Data Science for All (DS4A)** fellowship — Team 29.

## Research Question

How did COVID-19 affect academic outcomes across New York City's five largest school districts, and what role did school funding, demographics, and attendance patterns play in shaping those outcomes?

## Overview

As AI and technology reshape education, understanding how institutional factors — funding equity, demographic composition, attendance — predict academic performance becomes increasingly critical. This project examined those relationships across NYC public schools from 2013–2022, with a focus on the disruption introduced by COVID-19.

## Analyses

- **School Funding & Math Outcomes** — Examined the relationship between per-pupil funding and state math test results across middle schools
- **Attendance & SHSAT Admissions** — Analyzed whether attendance patterns predict access to NYC's specialized high schools (a proxy for educational opportunity)
- **Attendance & State Math Test Scores** — Correlated attendance trends with academic performance
- **Demographic Snapshot** — Profiled school-level racial and socioeconomic composition from 2013–2022
- **Admissions Trends (2013–2021)** — Tracked SHSAT offer patterns by sending school over time

## Data Sources

- NYC Department of Education demographic snapshots (2013–2022)
- SHSAT admissions offers by sending school (2015–2021)
- Daily attendance by school (2013–2021)
- State math test results (2015–2022)
- Middle school per-pupil funding data (scraped from NYC DOE)

## Tech Stack

- **Analysis:** Python, pandas, numpy, matplotlib
- **Data Collection:** Custom web scraper (Python) for school funding data
- **Environment:** Google Colab, Jupyter Notebooks
